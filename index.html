Community Guidelines

Thanks for joining our furry community!

On behalf of all of the admins/moderators, we sincerely hope you enjoy your time joining our events, joining our group chats, joining our Mastodon instance, and interacting with us in general. Here are just a few things you should keep in mind when being a part of our community.

Basic Guidelines

Here are some sections to outline the basic rules of our community.

Just Be A Furry!

Please feel free to be yourself! We don’t want anyone in the community to feel censored or like they have to be someone they’re not.

However, we will not condone[1]:

  • Threats of any kind.
  • Bullying or insulting anyone in the community.
  • Continued unwanted sexual or romantic advances, whether public or in private.

Additionally, the following incidents will be reported[2]:

  • Any pictures, videos, media, or discussion of illegal activities (inside the jurisdiction of the respective region the activity was committed).
  • Death threats or suicidal threats.

Get Involved!

We would love for you to get more involved in our community by coming to any planned or on-the-fly scheduled events! Just remember that the restrictions listed above still apply in person. In addition, you should keep these in mind:

  1. Respect other furs’ personal space. Everyone loves hugs and cuddles (for the most part)! However, not everyone wants them at any given point in time. Make sure you always have consent and ask if you are unsure if you’re making someone uncomfortable. FURSUITING IS NOT CONSENT.
  2. Keep it civil. If you do lose your cool, that’s ok, it happens to the best of us. As outlined earlier, please refrain from personally insulting anyone or making threats.
  3. Report bad behavior. Your admins and moderators are your friends. If someone makes you feel uncomfortable, or does  or says something you think they shouldn’t have, please report it. We can’t help the community be a better place if we don’t know there’s a problem!

What’s Considered NSFW? (What’s Considered SFW?)

In general, things that are considered Not Safe For Work would be anything that would otherwise be deemed inappropriate to view or say in a public, school, or work setting. Some examples are, but are not limited to:

  • Depictions of sexual organs, implied sexual content, or any implied rule 34 related content.
  • Depictions of excessive gore, violence, or profanity.
  • Discussion or implied discussion of sexual activities, innuendos, gore, or kink related activity.

Most offenses of this nature will be dealt with a warning, and the offender in question will be asked to move or remove the post(s) in question.

Forum Specific Guidelines

These are some guidelines that are specific to their respective forums.

OwO What’s This?...

Our community offers a few NSFW forums. If you are inclined to join them, then please keep the following in mind, in addition to the Basic Guidelines:

  1. We don’t want to notice your bulge. Do not post explicit media featuring yourself or anyone in the chat. These are not open forums to show everyone your dick pics.
  2. “Don’t meme too hard.” Posting NSFW jokes and memes are fine! Just please don’t spam the forum with it.
  3. We aren’t a dating service. Our NSFW forums are not public forums to hook up on. If you are looking to do that, please keep it in DMs.

Political Discussion

Not everyone in our community likes to engage in political discussions. For that reason, we provide a forum specifically for such situations. In additional to the Basic Guidelines, you should keep the following in mind:

  1. “Please be respectful of each other. Debate is okay, fighting is not.” We want to encourage healthy debate of everyone’s political views, creating an environment in which we can all learn from and be exposed to each others’ political views.
  2. Facism, white supremacy, and other hateful views will not be tolerated. While we really want to encourage everyone to discuss their own political views, we will not tolerate hateful views. There is no budging on this, and if you are unsure whether we will be tolerable of your views, consider contacting an admin first. If you choose to do so anonymously, we will respond in the form of a formal announcement in the forum.
  3. The political chat is not an NSFW forum. This is not a place for you to discuss your NSFW political views or lifestyles. That discussion should be kept in our NSFW specific forums.

Administration & Moderation

Everything you should need to know about how we moderate the community and how you can help us make the community a better place.

The Admins’/Moderators’ Job

First and foremost, our community Admins/Moderators are here to help make the community a better place. The primary job we are here to do is to moderate the chat when discussions get out of hand, enforce the guidelines listed throughout this document, and respond to reports and issues raised by the community members.

All this being said, the admins/moderators are not dictators, and should never:

  • Make you or any other member of the community feel singled out or belittled.
  • Give their opinions on a matter or situation as if it were law.
  • Carry out unnecessary punishments, deletion of comments, kicks, bans, etc.
  • Act or present themselves as though they are above or do not need to adhere to the community guidelines.
  • Ignore or dismiss legitimate[3] criticism, suggestion, or discussion of how the community is being moderated.

When a discussion gets out of hand, an admin/moderator may:

  • Step in to ask the discussion to be moved to DMs, or to another forum.
  • Warn that the discussion is inappropriate for the current forum or otherwise violates our community guidelines. This may result in the request and/or deletion of offending posts.
  • Ask that the discussion stop and that the current forum move on to another topic.

Using the Telegram Adminbot

Basic usage is outlined in the “Reporting a bad fur” section 2. We made this bot to help the community feel more comfortable with reporting issues and to make it easier for us to receive reports. With that in mind, here are some ways you should not use the adminbot and will be punished for doing so:

  1. Do not spam. This should be the most obvious. When we say spam, it means don’t send messages to the adminbot that are unnecessary. If the report really is long, then it is understandable if you simply need to say all of what you’re sending. However, if you are just straight forwarding a long conversation from the group chat, it might be more worth it to simply ping us in the chat with the /admin command if you feel comfortable doing so.
  2. No unnecessary messages or pinging. Similar to number 1, the adminbot is not a service to use that gives you a straight gateway to communicate with the admins about anything. You should really only use this tool if you are reporting an issue related to the community. If you have suggestions or commentary on what is going on, please keep it to a minimum. The adminbot is not a suggestion box. Additionally, unnecessary amounts of using the /admin command will not be tolerated. When you ping all of the admins, we expect to be responding to an issue, so please really consider whether what is going on requires our attention before throwing out the command.

If you’re interested in seeing the source code for the bot we use, want to use it for your own Telegram chats, or want to contribute to its development, here are the related links:

  • Github repo: https://github.com/asonix/telegram-admin-bot 
  • Crates.io package: https://crates.io/crates/admin_bot 

Reporting A Bad Fur

We get it. It’s no fun having to call someone out for their behavior. For that reason, we provide a couple ways that you can notify our admins/moderators when someone breaks the guidelines/rules.

  1. Contact an admin/moderator directly. This one is probably the most simple option. You can always DM any of our admins/moderators directly if you want them to be notified of an issue. Additionally, simply @ing an admin/moderator will allow us to know that an offense took place.
  2. Use the adminbot. If you are in one of our Telegram chats, you can use the adminbot to send anonymous reports to the admins. A quick rundown of how to do this:
  1. Using the /admins command will automatically ping all of the admins in the respective chat.
  2. Sending a DM to the @coconutsadminbot will automatically and anonymously forward any messages to a specific chat that only the admins can access.
  3. Any forwarded messages to the @coconutsadminbot in a DM will also be forwarded to a specific chat that only the admins can access, along with who originally send that forwarded message. You will remain anonymous.

Consequences of Bad Behavior

In general, minor offenses (especially a first one) will be dealt with a warning being given by an admin/moderator and possibly removal of the offending post(s). In most cases, we will ask that you remove the offending post(s) or move them yourself. If they are not removed within 15 minutes of the request, an admin/moderator will remove them.

Keep in mind that continuous violations of the community guidelines and/or respective forum’s rules will most likely result in a kick or ban. In most cases, this will require the agreement of the majority of active admins/moderators. However, in extreme cases, admins/moderators may kick or ban members without doing so (See section 2 in “Just be a furry!”.).

Since incidences can vary in intensity and variety, respective consequences are up to the admins’/moderators’ discretion.

Challenging an Admin/Moderator

As outlined previously in “The Admins’/Moderators’ Job” section, the admins/moderators are not dictators. Their say on things is not law. If you, or members of the chat, have a disagreement on the way a certain situation is handled, you may do the following:

  1. Personally message an admin/moderator explaining your disagreement with the actions taken, and your side on the matter. This will not guarantee that the punishment will be lifted or reversed, but will make the admins/moderators aware that there is a disagreement.
  1. If there are numerous people who disagree with a decision that the admins/moderators have made, please include everyone you know has a disagreement in your initial message. We will contact everyone listed to hear what they have to say.
  2. Regardless of whether the punishment is reversed or lifted, an admin/moderator will make a public statement in the forums that there was a disagreement and the resulting decision from that disagreement. This will ensure that everyone in the chat knows we acknowledged that a member(s) had a dissenting view on our actions.
  3. We will keep those who brought up the disagreement anonymous, unless they specifically state that they want the community to know they had a disagreement. This ensures that we have both privacy with reports and clarity with our members.
  1. DM the adminbot explaining your disagreement with the actions taken, and your side on the matter. This will result in the same response outlined in section 1.
  2. Publicly express your grievances with our decision in the forums. This is not recommended, as it will likely spark debate within the community about their opinions on the matter and is difficult to moderate. However, if it happens, the following response should be made:
  1. An admin/moderator will made a public statement in the forum acknowledging that there was a disagreement and that it is being taken into consideration.
  2. The member expressing their disagreement will be DMed to allow them to express their full view on the matter (if they feel they need to say anything else).
  3. Any other members who publicly express their disagreement with our decision will also be DMed.
  4. Regardless of whether the punishment is reversed or lifted, an admin/moderator will make a public statement in the forums that there was a disagreement and the resulting decision from that disagreement.
  5. In such a case, we will continue to preserve anonymity when possible.
