Community Guidelines

Thanks for joining our furry community!

On behalf of all of the admins/moderators, we sincerely hope you enjoy your time joining our events, joining our group chats, joining our Mastodon instance, and interacting with us in general. Here are just a few things you should keep in mind when being a part of our community.

Just be a furry!

Please feel free to be yourself! We don’t want anyone in the community to feel censored or like they have to be someone they’re not.

However, we will not condone[1]:

  • Threats of any kind.
  • Bullying or insulting anyone in the community.
  • Continued unwanted sexual or romantic advances, whether public or in private.

Additionally, the following incidents will be reported[2]:

  • Any pictures, videos, media, or discussion of illegal activities (inside the jurisdiction of the respective region the activity was committed).
  • Death threats or suicidal threats.

Get involved!

We would love for you to get more involved in our community by coming to any planned or on-the-fly scheduled events! Just remember that the restrictions listed above still apply in person. In addition, you should keep these in mind:

  1. Respect other furs’ personal space. Everyone loves hugs and cuddles (for the most part)! However, not everyone wants them at any given point in time. Make sure you always have consent and ask if you are unsure if you’re making someone uncomfortable. FURSUITING IS NOT CONSENT.
  2. Keep it civil. If you do lose your cool, that’s ok, it happens to the best of us. As outlined earlier, please refrain from personally insulting anyone or making threats.
  3. Report bad behavior. Your admins and moderators are your friends. If someone makes you feel uncomfortable, or does  or says something you think they shouldn’t have, please report it. We can’t help the community be a better place if we don’t know there’s a problem!

Mastodon

Please adhere to the guidelines listed here as well as the rules listed on our instance.

Reporting a bad fur

We get it. It’s no fun having to call someone out for their behavior. For that reason, we provide a couple ways that you can notify our admins/moderators when someone breaks the guidelines/rules.

  1. Contact an admin/moderator directly. This one is probably the most simple option. You can always DM any of our admins/moderators directly if you want them to be notified of an issue. Additionally, simply @ing an admin/moderator will allow us to know that an offense took place.
  2. Use the adminbot. If you are in one of our Telegram chats, you can use the adminbot to send anonymous reports to the admins. A quick rundown of how to do this:
  1. Using the /admins command will automatically ping all of the admins in the respective chat.
  2. Sending a DM to the @coconutsadminbot will automatically and anonymously forward any messages to a specific chat that only the admins can access.
  3. Any forwarded messages to the @coconutsadminbot in a DM will also be forwarded to a specific chat that only the admins can access, along with who originally send that forwarded message. You will remain anonymous.

Consequences from bad behavior

In general, minor offenses (especially a first one) will be dealt with a warning being given by an admin/moderator and possibly removal of the offending post(s). In most cases, we will ask that you remove the offending post(s) or move them yourself. If they are not removed within 15 minutes of the request, an admin/moderator will remove them.

Keep in mind that continuous violations of the community guidelines and/or respective forum’s rules will most likely result in a kick or ban. In most cases, this will require the agreement of the majority of active admins/moderators. However, in extreme cases, admins/moderators may kick or ban members without doing so (See section 2 in “Just be a furry!”.).

Since incidences can vary in intensity and variety, respective consequences are up to the admins’/moderators’ discretion.

[1] These incidents will result in a warning in most cases, but can in extreme circumstances lead to a ban.

[2] These incidents will result in kicking from any/all community related forums and in extreme cases will lead to a ban. We will also report incidents of this kind to the proper authorities.
